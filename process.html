How we work

Clear vehicle moving processes for every transport request.

From self-drive delivery to trailer, truck and flatbed transport, every move is planned around collection, inspection, transport and handover.

Transport workflows

Choose the right moving process for the vehicle and route.

Each workflow keeps the request, allocation, inspection, transport and final handover clear from the start.

01

Self-Drive Process

For driveable vehicles moved by an allocated driver.

  1. RequestThe customer requests to move a vehicle from point A to point B.
  2. AllocationThe vehicle is allocated to a driver.
  3. InspectionThe driver inspects the vehicle and collects it.
  4. TransportThe driver transports the vehicle from point A to point B.
  5. DeliveryAt the destination, the driver cleans the vehicle and completes delivery.
02

Trailer Process

For vehicles moved securely on a trailer.

  1. RequestThe customer requests to move a vehicle from point A to point B.
  2. AllocationA transport vehicle and driver are allocated for the request.
  3. LoadingThe driver inspects the vehicle, loads it onto the trailer and straps it down.
  4. TransportThe driver transports the vehicle to the destination area.
  5. CleaningThe driver drops the vehicle at a car wash or car detailer.
  6. Final DeliveryThe vehicle is strapped onto the trailer again and delivered to the specific destination.
03

Truck Transport Process

For run and non-run vehicles moved through a depot process.

  1. PlanningTruck transport is planned as either a non-run or run vehicle movement.
  2. AccessUnlike flatbeds, trucks do not arrive on-site to collect the vehicle directly.
  3. Flatbed LinkA flatbed first transports the vehicle to the truck or depot.
  4. Depot LoadingThe vehicle is moved to a depot and loaded onto the truck using equipment such as a forklift.
  5. TransportThe truck transports the vehicle toward the destination route.
04

Flatbed Transport Process

For direct pickup, secure loading and destination handover.

  1. RequestThe customer requests to move a vehicle from point A to point B.
  2. AllocationA flatbed vehicle and driver are allocated for the request.
  3. InspectionThe driver inspects the vehicle before loading.
  4. LoadingThe vehicle is loaded onto the flatbed and secured for transport.
  5. DeliveryThe driver transports the vehicle and completes delivery at the destination.
Operational details

Every handover is managed with the right vehicle checks and delivery notes.

Whether the vehicle is self-driven, moved by trailer, handled through a truck depot or carried on a flatbed, the process keeps collection details, vehicle condition and destination timing visible.

  • Point A and point B confirmed before allocation
  • Driver inspection before collection or loading
  • Cleaning or detailing included where the process requires it
  • Final handover at the agreed destination
